Every era leaves a layer the Earth remembers — ash, ice, coral, stone. Ours is being written right now, in carbon and heat and choices. Human Legacy is a record of that layer, and a case for changing what goes in it.

A CORE SAMPLE OF THE ATMOSPHERE · 1850 → 2026
Why this exists

Geologists will one day read our decades the way we read ice cores and tree rings — as data. The plastics in the sediment. The carbon in the air. The species that vanished on our watch. That record is being written by ordinary decisions, made by billions of people, every single day. This site exists to make that record legible — and to make clear that the next layer is still ours to choose.

02 — The Impact

What has actually changed, in terms that hold still.

Percentages and parts-per-million are hard to feel. Here's the same story in units that are easier to picture — then a real explanation, in two minutes, from people who study this for a living.

🌡️
1850→+1.3°C
Global average temperature, today

Sounds small. It isn't — it's the difference between the climate humans built civilization in and one with measurably more heatwaves, wildfires, and extreme rain worldwide.

🌊
1900→+21 cm
Global mean sea level, today

About the height of a shoebox. Enough to make ordinary storm surges reach further inland and turn once-rare coastal flooding into a regular occurrence in many cities.

🧊
1980→−13%
Arctic sea ice, per decade

Roughly a tenth of the summer ice cap gone every ten years. Less white surface to reflect sunlight means the region absorbs more heat, which melts more ice — a feedback loop, not a one-time change.

04 — Solutions

What actually fixes this.

A mix of things you can start today, and the bigger structural shifts that move the number fastest.

Individual — starts today

Cut your highest-leverage habit, not every habit

Flights, car miles, and red meat move a personal footprint faster than almost anything else. Pick the one that fits your life and change that one thing well, rather than trying to optimize everything at once.

See your numbers below ↓
Household — this year

Electrify what burns fossil fuel at home

Heat pumps instead of gas furnaces, induction instead of gas stoves, and a green energy tariff where available. These are one-time swaps that keep paying off every year after, with no ongoing willpower required.

Buildings: 5% of global emissions
Collective — where the biggest wins live

Push for grid decarbonization and carbon pricing

Energy and industry together are well over half of global emissions, and they're largely decided by utilities, regulators, and industrial policy — not individual purchases. Voting, public comment, and organized pressure move this faster than any personal choice.

Energy + industry: ~58% of global emissions
Collective — proven and scaling

Renewables are now the cheapest new power in most places

Solar and wind costs have fallen dramatically over the past decade, to the point that building new renewable capacity is now often cheaper than running existing coal plants. The economics increasingly favor the fix — the bottleneck is deployment speed, permitting, and grid capacity, not invention.

Cost curve favors clean energy
